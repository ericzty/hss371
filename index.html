The ARPANET

The ARPANET

The U.S. Advanced Research Projects Agency Network (ARPANET) was the first public packet-switched computer network. It was first used in 1969 and finally decommissioned in 1989. ARPANET's main use was for academic and research purposes. (wikipedia)

The internet was still at it's infancy, people still only talked to each other in real life

Internet Relay Chat

Internet Relay Chat

Internet Relay Chat (IRC) is a text-based chat system for instant messaging. IRC is designed for group communication in discussion forums, called channels, but also allows one-on-one communication via private messages as well as chat and data transfer, including file sharing. (wikipedia)

Bulletin board system

Bulletin board system

A bulletin board system (BBS), also called a computer bulletin board service (CBBS),was a computer server running software that allowed users to connect to the system using a terminal program. Once logged in, the user can perform functions such as uploading and downloading software and data, reading news and bulletins, and exchanging messages with other users through public message boards and sometimes via direct chatting (wikipedia)

Image Boards

Image Boards

An imageboard is a type of Internet forum that focuses on the posting of images, often alongside text and discussion. The first imageboards were created in Japan as an extension of the textboard concept. (wikipedia)

Modern Web Forums

Modern Web Forums

Modern web forums have evolved to become more than just a place for discussion; they are now a vibrant social medium that fosters knowledge sharing, collaboration, and community building. When compared to the historical social mediums like ones discussed earlier, modern forums have advanced in terms of usability and interactive capabilities, providing a more engaging experience for users. While BBS acted as electronic bulletin boards for posting public messages, and IRCs allowed real-time text over the internet, they lacked in persistent conversation tracking, and user-friendly interfaces for the web browser. They also combine public group discussions with personal messaging, while also embedding gamification and rich text formatting, which encourages user engagement and community growth. Today, web forums, such as Reddit and Quora, represent not only tools for communication but also mediums for social influence and information exchange on a global scale.

Tiktok, Reels, Shorts

Tiktok, Reels, Shorts

With the rise of video-sharing platforms such as TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts has dramatically transformed the way we consume and engage with online content, marking a significant evolution in social media platforms. TikTok, Reels, and Shorts offer a creative and dynamic way to share and consume information in fast-paced, tightly edited videos. Unlike older social media like Facebook or Twitter that rely heavily on text and static images, these platforms cater to our heightened desire for quick entertainment and instant gratification. There are also great concern for how it impacts our cognitive abilities.

Bigger Social Mediums

Bigger Social Mediums

Discord represents a significant shift in online communication and community building, marking a powerful evolution from traditional forums and chat platforms. It is very popular for it's text, voice, and video capabilities. This platform integrates public channels for group discussions and private spaces for personal chats, making it a hybrid of the open collaboration of web forums and the private networking of traditional social platforms. Functionally, it appears as an upgrade from the IRC, but with more user-friendly interfaces, advanced multimedia integration, and specific gaming-friendly features. It is also very popular in school communities with topic focused servers.

Methods

Findings

We show that the past 2 decades have seen only small and inconsistent changes in global well-being and mental health that are not suggestive of the idea that the adoption of Internet and mobile broadband is consistently linked to negative psychological outcomes. (Vuorre, M., & Przybylski, A. K. (2023))

Findings

Filter bubbles do not run our lives. Personalized filtering by algorithms is not the cause for public opinion formation and has merely trivial effects on search results with major search engines. The concept is mainly used as a metaphor to reduce the complexity of social, economic and technological dynamics on platforms and public debates, but is of little value beyond that. (Sebastian Randerath. Myth #22: We all live in filter bubbles. 2019)

Findings

Overall, the available evidence indicates that the Internet can produce both acute and sustained alterations in each of these areas of cognition, which may be reflected in changes in the brain. However, an emerging priority for future research is to determine the effects of extensive online media usage on cognitive development in youth, and examine how this may differ from cognitive outcomes and brain impact of uses of Internet in the elderly. (Firth J, Torous J, et al. 2019)

Smaller Communities

Irc, bbs, forums, small discord servers (has always been around)

  • Provides a more personal and intimate social platform. (Similar to real life)
  • Encourages in-depth and focused conversations on specific topics.
  • Allows users to form closer knit and supportive communities since they share common interests.
  • Often moderated well by the community.

Bigger Communities

Twitter, Large Discord Servers, image boards (relatively new concept)

  • Allows users to connect with a larger and more diverse audience. (New to us humans as well)
  • Provides more exposure, making it optimal for marketing, promotions or spreading awareness about various subjects.
  • Suitable for light and casual conversations due to the pace and volume of conversations.
  • Structured around user-generated content which promotes creativity and engagement.
  • Less moderated.
